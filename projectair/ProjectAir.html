Effects of air pollution

Children, pregnant women and the elderly are the most affected of all, due to air pollution

When pregnant women are exposed to polluted air, they are more likely to give birth prematurely, and have small, low birth-weight children.

WHO estimates that in 2016, 600,000 children died from acute lower respiratory infections caused by polluted air.

Long-term exposure to air pollution contributes to the progression of heart disease by accelerating atherosclerosis, a major cause of heart attack and stroke.
